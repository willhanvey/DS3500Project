III. RECOMMENDAION OF THE FIRST COMMITTEE

10. The First committee recommends to the General Assembly the adoption of the
following draft resolution:

Establishment of a nuclear-weapon-free zone in South Asia

The General Assembly,

Recalling its resolutions 3265 B (XXIX) of 9 December 1974, 3476 B (xxx)
of 11 December 1975, 31/73 of 10 December 1976, 32/83 of 12 December 1977,
33/65 of 14 December 1978, 34/78 of 11 December 1979, 35/148 of

15 December 1983, 39/55 of 12 December 1984 and 40/83 of 12 December 1985
concerning the establishment of a nuclear-weapon-free Zone in South Asia,

Reiterating its conviction that the establishment of nuclear-weapon-free
zones in various regions of the world is one of the measures which can
contribute effectively to the objectives of non-proliferation of nuclear
weapons and qeneral and complete disarmament,

Believing that the establishment of a nuclear-weapon-free zone in South
Asia, as in other regions, will assist in the strenqtheninq of the security of
the States of the region against the use or threat of use of nuclear weapons,

Noting with appreciation the declarations issued at the highest level  by
Governments of South Asian States that are developing their peaceful nuclear
proqrantmes and reaffirming their undertaking not to acquire or manufacture
nU@lear  weapons and to devote their nuclear programmes exclusively to the
economic and social advancement of their peoples,
