PROGRAMME BUDGET FOR THE BIENNIUM 1982-1983 

Report of the Fifth Committee 

RaPporteur: Mr. Even FONTAINE ORTIZ (Cuba) 

1. At its 3rd plenary meeting, on 23 September 1983, the General Assembly decided 
to include in its agenda the item entitled "Programme budget for the biennium 
1982-1983". At its 4th plenary meeting, on the same day, the Assembly decided to 
allocate the item to the Fifth Committee. 

2. The Committee considered this item at its 6lst, 69th and 73rd meetings, on 12, 
16 and 19 December. It had before it the following documents: 

(a) Report of the Advisory Committee on Administrative and Budgetary 
Questions on the Office of the United Nations Disaster Relief Co-ordinator: grants 
for emergency disaster assistance (A/38/476)J 

(b) Report of the Advisory Committee on Administrative and Budgetary 
Questions on training and research: united Nations Institute for Training and 
Research (A/38/7/Add.20)J 

(c) Report of the Secretary-General on the programme budget performance of 
the United Nations for the biennium 1982-1983 (A/C.S/38/49 and Add.l-33) and the 
related report of the Advisory Committee (A/38/748). 

3. At its 6lst meeting, on 12 December, the Committee approved, without a vote, 
an additional appropriation of $240,000 under section 22 of the programme budget 
for the biennium 1982-1983. 

4. At its 69th meeting, on 16 December, the Committee decided that the advance to 
UNITAR provided for in draft resolution A/C.2/38/L.47/Rev.l should be financed by 
an additional appropriation of $886,000 under a temporary section 33 of the 
programme budget for the biennium 1982-1983.

5. At its 73rd meeting, on 19 December, the Committee decided, inter alia, to 
authorize the Secretary-General to enter into commitments of up to $1,000,000 for 
1984, for the resources required to initiate the activities called for in 
section II, paragraph 5, of the draft resolution recommended by the Second 
Committee (A/38/702/Add.3, para. 18, draft resolution I), to be drawn from savings 
in the 1982-1983 programme budget. 

6. At the same meeting, the Committee decided, by 58 votes to 13, with 
8 abstentions, to recommend that the General Assembly should approve the revised 
estimates tor the biennium 1982-1983 in the amounts shown under the revised 
estimates columns in tables 1 and 2 of annex I to the Secretary-General's report 
(A/C.S/38/49), except that the amount for section 22 would be increased by 
$240,000, that an amount of $886,000 would be included under a temporary section 33 
entitled "Advance to UNITAR", and that the total for the expenditure sections would 
be increased accordingly. 

7. At the same meeting, the Committee decided, without objection, to request the 
Rapporteur to reflect the decisions taken by the Committee under agenda item 108 in 
the form of draft resolutions to be presented for approval by the General Assembly. 

8. Comments and observations made in the course of the discussion of this 
question are reflected in the summary records of the meetings (see A/C.5/38/SR.61, 
69 and 73).
